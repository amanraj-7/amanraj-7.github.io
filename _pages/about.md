I am a Sustainable Engineering PhD student at the [School of Sustainable Engineering and Built Environment (SSEBE)](https://ssebe.engineering.asu.edu/?gad_source=1&gad_campaignid=23539904360&gbraid=0AAAAA-TKE_6AhLAM8LCgRsidFztb2ZP8t&gclid=Cj0KCQjwve7NBhC-ARIsALZy9HVtZxtWTW2Ep-Hg6q_CKr8oGWr5KZBTklwOFG28Hkc8hQukopyCMoAaAin7EALw_wcB), [Arizona State University](https://www.asu.edu/), under the supervision of [Dr Dwarak Ravikumar](https://search.asu.edu/profile/1869256) in the [Circular Economy, Sustainable Energy And Materials (CESEM) lab](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=LV3kAbYAAAAJ&hl=en). My research integrates advanced imaging technique with machine learning technique to develop a novel non-contact and non-destructive diagnostic method for photovoltaic (PV) module health, enabling higher-value circular economy pathways for the PV industry. My other work involves also integrating life cycle assessment (LCA) and geospatial supply chain optimization models to quantify environmental trade-offs and decarbonize solar PV recycling pathways.

Before joining ASU, I earned my M.Sc. in Sustainable Systems from the [Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT)](https://www.rit.edu/), where I worked with [Dr. Callie Babbitt](https://www.rit.edu/directory/cwbgis-callie-babbitt) at the [Golisano Institute for Sustainability](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tiUrxZLNB1c&list=PLnnkG4saHTez5MtJ-PYVlOeBYG0Gcvs1S&index=1). My research there centered on the life cycle impact assessment of lithium-ion battery remanufacturing for second-life applications — examining material recovery efficiencies and the environmental trade-offs embedded in various reuse pathways.

My academic journey began in India, where I completed an M.Sc. in Public Policy at the [Tata Institute of Social Sciences](https://tiss.ac.in/) under the guidance of [Dr. Chandrashekhar Joglekar](https://tiss.ac.in/view/9/employee/chandrashekhar-joglekar/), and a B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering at [VIT University, Vellore](https://vit.ac.in/). This interdisciplinary foundation — spanning engineering, policy, and systems thinking — continues to shape how I approach complex sustainability challenges.

When my brain is not *braining*, you’ll likely find me playing, watching, or diving deep into tactical analysis, of the world of football (US soccer). I'm an avid Manchester United fan (GGMU!). I also love a perfectly brewed cup of coffee. Beyond this, I love anime (One Piece) and a constant seeker of new music.

As my good friend  [Dr. Pulkit](https://pulkit-khandelwal.github.io/) says, “we believe in [**Slow Science**](http://slow-science.org/)” — meaning the website is evolving at a peer-review pace 😄
